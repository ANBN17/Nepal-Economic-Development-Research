# Nepal Economic Development Research

Policy research project focused on governance reform, economic transformation, and district-level development strategies for Nepal.

## Overview

Nepal faces several structural challenges including weak project execution, corruption risks in public procurement, limited industrial development, and heavy dependence on remittances. This project aims to explore practical policy solutions for sustainable economic development and institutional reform.

The research focuses on three key themes:

- Governance reform and public sector efficiency
- Sector-based economic development
- District and constituency level economic opportunities

## Research Scope

This project will gradually develop a comprehensive economic framework covering:

- 77 Districts of Nepal
- 165 Parliamentary Constituencies
- Key economic sectors
- Institutional governance reforms

## Policy Framework

Initial recommendations focus on:

- Digital governance and anti-corruption reforms
- Fiscal discipline and efficient public spending
- SME and entrepreneurship development
- Agro-processing and tourism industries
- Renewable energy and hydropower development
- Local economic clustering

## Political Context

As part of preliminary research, the manifesto of the Rastriya Swatantra Party (RSP) was also reviewed to understand current reform priorities in Nepal. Many broad policy ideas align with the recommendations presented here, particularly around governance reform, job creation, and economic modernization. However, this research focuses more on practical economic implementation and long-term development planning.

## Future Research

Upcoming research will explore:

- District-wise economic opportunities
- Constituency-level development strategies
- Local economic clustering
- Sectoral growth models
- Fiscal feasibility and investment strategy
